## What do you take home?

The chain, one more time. A song and a taste became two tangles of qualities. The tangles folded into arrows. Multiply-and-add collapsed two arrows into one score, and the score turned out to be a meter: agree, ignore, oppose, with one crack in the glass where size can shout over alignment.

One more aisle before you go, just to make sure the recipe really travels: score yourself against a job posting. The posting lists what the role wants. You list what you bring. The same multiply-and-add folds the match into one number for the fit, and nothing in the arithmetic knows the aisle changed.

Your turn to build one. Pick any two things you could describe with the same short list of numbers: you and a city, a recipe and a pantry, a workout and a week. Write both lists, multiply the matching entries, add. Then say what a high score, a zero, and a negative number would each mean for your pair. If you can read all three, the meter is yours.
