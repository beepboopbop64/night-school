## What did we just build?

Pull back from the sweep and look at what your hands actually learned to do. Point the probe along a candidate, and the sum climbs. Point it across, and the sum dies to zero. Point it against, and the sum goes negative. That's not three separate behaviors. It's one dial, and you've been turning it the whole time.

One multiply-and-add, run once, and it already reads like a meter: agree on one side, ignore in the middle, oppose on the other. No extra machinery needed. The direction of the probe is the only thing that ever moved, and while every arrow held the same length, direction was all the sum ever responded to.

So let's name it plainly. The dot product is a similarity meter. Two lists of numbers go in, a is the probe's list, b is the candidate's, and one reading of alignment comes out.

Before you scroll on: positive, zero, negative. Say what each reading claims about two vectors, in your own words.
