## What did we just build?

Step back from the sweep for a second and look at what your hands actually learned to do. Point the probe along a candidate, and the sum climbs. Point it across, and the sum dies to zero. Point it against, and the sum goes negative. That's not three separate behaviors. It's one dial, and you've been turning it the whole time.

One multiply-and-add, run once, and it already reads like a meter: agree on one side, ignore in the middle, oppose on the other. No extra machinery needed. The direction of the probe is the only thing that ever moved, and the direction is the only thing the sum ever responded to.

So let's name it plainly. The dot product is a similarity meter. Two lists of numbers go in, and one honest reading of alignment comes out.

That reading isn't vague or approximate. It tracks direction exactly, because direction is all the sweep ever changed. Agree, ignore, oppose: that's the whole dial, and you built it with nothing but multiplication and addition.
