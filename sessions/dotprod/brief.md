# Session brief: dotprod (the dot product as similarity)

## The aha, and how it could be wrong

**"The dot product is a similarity meter."** Falsifiable, and the session
must earn it by hand: point a probe arrow along a candidate and the
multiply-and-add score climbs; point across and it reads zero; point against
and it goes negative. If the score failed to track alignment, the sentence
would be decoration.

The honest edge that seeds the breadcrumb: the raw score also rewards
length. A meter that confuses loudness with agreement is not done being
designed, and that gap is the walk-home question (it points straight at
normalization, and later at scaled attention scores).

## Why this session exists

M6 (the round-trip) needs a small, real session to push through every
production stage S4a to S7 plus a synthetic revision round, before the
attention pilot spends Jake's attention. Four beats, one interactive, one
notebook. Everything contracted, nothing decorative.
